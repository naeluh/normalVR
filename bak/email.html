Dan Budnik - Marching To The Freedom Dream

By Trolley Books

In 1963, photographer Dan Budnik went to Washington D.C. to document a civil rights march. That day became one of the most famous in modern American history, culminating in Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have A Dream" speech. To commemorate the 50th anniversary of the march, Budnik is publishing a new book with his photos from that day. 

FUBAR: MOTHER RUSSIA

By Jeff McComsey

Two years ago, graphic novelist Jeff McComsley's FUBAR 2 went from Kickstarter project to the New York Times Best Sellers List. Jeff's now back with a new graphic novel, Mother Russia. It takes place in 1943 in Stalingrad, where zombies have taken the city hostage and the only hope for the people is a single baby with a long rifle. 

Rainbow Pencils

By Duncan Shotton

Pencils don't just write on paper; they can also be made of it. Duncan Shotton figured this out, and has been working to create a new pencil made entirely from recycled paper. His adorable project, Rainbow Pencils, also unravels in lovely rainbow shapes when sharpened.
